## Overview

The Grinn AstraSOM-1680, powered by the Synaptics Astra SL1680 and running Avocado OS, delivers a production-grade edge AI platform purpose-built for smart camera and computer vision deployments. With 7.9 TOPS of NPU performance, dual MIPI CSI camera interfaces, and HDMI 2.1 input in a compact 37x42.6mm LGA footprint, the AstraSOM-1680 provides the compute density needed for real-time vision workloads at the edge.

Avocado OS eliminates the 18+ months of custom Linux infrastructure work that typically stands between a working prototype and a shippable product. Application engineers can begin iterating on vision models from day one with a validated BSP, atomic OTA updates, and CRA-compliant CVE tracking built in. Together, Grinn and Peridio provide a complete, programmable foundation for industrial-grade vision systems that can move seamlessly from lab to volume production.

## Use Cases

### Smart Camera Deployments

Deploy 7.9 TOPS of NPU performance for real-time object detection, classification, and tracking in compact smart camera form factors. Dual MIPI CSI inputs enable stereo vision or simultaneous visible/IR capture for industrial inspection and security applications.

### Off-Grid Edge AI Vision

Ultra-low-power standby architecture with sensor-based wake enables battery and solar-powered vision applications in remote locations. Process vision workloads on-device without continuous connectivity, uploading results when network access is available.

### Ruggedized Industrial Vision

Grinn's validated LGA196 module design with Avocado OS LTS support enables customers to move from prototype to volume production in quarters, not years. Compact form factor fits into IP-rated enclosures for harsh environment deployment.
